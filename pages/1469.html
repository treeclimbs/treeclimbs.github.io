Description & Commentary:

Introduced in 1992, DMM Mamba body was the first hot-forged aluminum alloy carabiner, a process which moves more material than when bent or cold-forging carabiner bodies. This helps designers maximize strength and minimize weight - common in today's world of high-performance climbnig carabiners.

First released in non-locking versions, DMM later produced a straight gate and screwlocker variant later on.

The Mamba body sports a deep, pointed basket with a rope guide groove, a bowed S-spine and captive eye designed for the webbing of a quickdraw. The clipping action is light and the bent gate beautifully matches the interior sweep of the spine when fully open. All these features aid in clipping the carabiner into a bolt or the rope into a carabiner when sport climbing - something certainly valued by rock climbers while leading a difficult pitch!

The nose has a narrow, full-length guard - better than nothing, but the gate and rivets still extend significantly beyond the guard. At the time of the Mamba's release (early 90's), a nose guard would have been relatively new feature. The rear of the gate is not shielded, but the captive eye should practically eliminate any crossloading and accompanying rope shredding.
