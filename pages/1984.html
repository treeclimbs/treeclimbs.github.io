Description & Commentary:

The Attache 3D was one of Petzl's first I-beam redesigns of their well established (and well liked) carabiners. For many years, they offered the new I-Beam profile Attache 3D alongside the older round profile Attache model, as the round profile can feed more smoothly and is longer wearing.

Eventually, Petzl discontinued the older models - leading some desperate Attache devotees to approach strangers at climbing sites about purchasing or trading for an under-appreciated first generation model made with full 12mm round stock.

The 3D retained the classic Attache shape, "Red-is-Dead" marker, debris clearning hole and aggressive sleeve knurling. Climbers appreciated the reduced weight and slim nose on remote climbs or at crowded belay stations, but many reported the redesigned gate with the distintive machined gate-stop jammed regularly after loading.

This example has a tapered/angled locking sleeve stop machined into the gate, unlike the a different version with a right-angle stop.
