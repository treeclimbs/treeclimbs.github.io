Description & Commentary:

The Kong Ergo Wire Lock is a low-security locking carabiner designed for Via Ferrata and adventure parks. The wire gate is blocked from opening by a spring-loaded lever positioned in the carabiner basket and wrapping around the interior and sides of the spine. The portion of the lever on the spine is wider and extends to the back edge of the spine.

By pressing on the back of this extended section, the lever pivots slightly, and permits the gate wire to exit the notch at the nose. The width and square edges of the locking lever's extended section combined with the light spring pressure and narrow spine make it easy to depress the lever just by pressing on the rear of the spine - the flesh of the finger or palm wraps around enough to move the lever the short distance required to unlock.

The lever and the position of the lever pivot has an added effect that the gate resists unlocking when a load is applied within the deep (spine-side) of the basket.

Kong's marketing material states that the lever is made from marine bronze, which helps the carabiner slide on the cable used in adventure parks or via ferrata routes. It also wears the difficult-to-replace cables less than a harder material such as stainless steel.

A plastic contact pad on the front of the gate positions the finger opposite the locking lever and the mechanism is comfortable to operate in many different pinching and squeezing positions. The contact pad can also be placed directly against a cable/bolt/object and clips easily by pressing on the back of the carabiner spine.

I have noticed that if the load is applied before the gate closes, the gate will be trapped slightly open until the load is briefly removed. This is more likely to happen when I manually open the gate, and release the gate after loading the basket.

Another way this can occur is by pressing the carabiner onto the object such that the gate opens all the way and the object presses against the lever along the interior spine. Once free of the gate, the gate begins closing, but the object/load continues to depress the lever as it slides into the basket - and blocking the gate from seating fully in the notch.

The Kong website lists a gate open strength of 8kN, but the body of the carabiner is not marked directly. Perhaps this is because the carabiner is marked EN12275:98, K-type connectors (klettersteig or via ferrata) did not require a gate open strength for this standard. EN12275:2013 require a minimum 8kN gate open strength and products with the UIAA label are also tested to 8kN over an edge (bending).
