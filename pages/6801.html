Description & Commentary:

The Kong Ergo Open Latch is a "stick clip" or "panic draw" carabiner that will hold the gate open until the latching lever is depressed by loading the carabiner basket.

When sport climbing (leading a bolted rock climbing route), a pole or "stick" is often used to clip the rope into the first bolt (or two) to help the climber avoid hitting the ground if they fall before reaching this bolt. The carabiner gate is held open with a special gadget or improvised tool such as duct tape or a small stick. Or a specialized carabiner such as this Ergo Open Latch, the Stubai Genius, or Madrock Trigger Wire (which uses a slightly different mechanism).

This style of carabiner can also be used on the end of a quickdraw for a quick "panic" clip after performing a hard (or scary) rock climbing move. Often the quickdraw sling is lengthened and and stiffened (with duct tape and/or a stick) to permit the climber to clip the bolt from a more secure position. Sort of a mini stick clip.

Kong sells the Ergo Open Latch as part of the Kong Panic quick draw, which includes a sling stiffened with a plastic tube inside the webbing.

Under load, the lever pivots to block the gate from opening, providing additional security.
