Description & Commentary:

A sub-ounce wiregate with a snagless (clean) nose. The Plume is Grivel's ultralight ultracompact line, also offered in standard (snagful) wiregate, wiregate with an internaal captive gate, Screw lock, Multi-gate, HMS (although body quite different), and wirelock (here's a Tau body w/ wirelock gate).

The wire gate aids in overcoming the one of the largest issues with compact carabiners - gate opening. At a 20.8mm gate opening, the Evo Wire has over 2mm additional gate clearance than the Plume Nut, but is still smaller than the gate openings of the comparable weight Petzl Ange S (23 mm) and Black Diamond Oz v2 (22 mm).

Most notibly, the nose of the Evo Wire extends completely past the sides of the gate (a full nose guard), as well as wrapping around one side of the gate. This half-hood helps avoid the nose from catching on objects when unclipping the carabiner and Grivel has designed a the asymmetric nose hook - rounding off the corner of the un-hooded side, further minimizing snags. The design appears to provide much of the benefits of a full-hood design with improved snow/ice/debris clearance.

The entire nose looks to be formed in the forging process, including the top and unshielded side of the hook. This leaves only the material between the side of the hook and the hood to be removed in a machining operation. The finishing process left a sharp inner-edge on the hood, which may cause increased wear on soft goods - especially if crossloaded.
