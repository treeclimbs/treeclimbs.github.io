Description & Commentary:

This oval appears to be very similar to some models of Bedayn carabiners, with the distinctive flattened ends from the bending process.

This is also almost identical to this DMC oval. Note center staked hinge pin, flush nose pin, notch angle and radiused interior of the gate at the hinge end.

The 1952 Ski Hut Catalog lists two carabiners, both Swiss, and does not mention Aluminum Alloy - certainly a selling point of the day. The 1953 catalog does list an aluminum oval, with no brand name given and an approximate breaking strength of 3000 lbs. The next catalog I have access to is 1966/67 which has a 3000 lbs aluminum alloy carabiner explicitly called the "Ski Hut Oval", a model advertised in 1968/69 and 1969/70 catalogs. The 1970/71 catalog lists a 2700 lbs carabiner with slightly different design, and no brand name.

The Ski Hut was an early outdoor recreation products retailer located in Berkeley, California. Ski Hut founder George Rudolph, also had an import focused company named Donner Mountain Corporation or DMC, which shared the same address.

Given Bedayn's connection to the SF Bay Area climbing / outdoors scene, SkiHut's prominant place in that scene, and the physical similarities between the carabiners, I feel pretty confident asserting that these were Bedayn white label production for Ski Hut.

More info on George Rudolph, Ski Hut, DMC (and Trailwise) can be found on (Mountain Hardwear co-Founder) Alan Tabor's Out in Under site, as well as historian Bruce Johnson's History of Gear.
