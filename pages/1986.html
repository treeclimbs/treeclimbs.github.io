Description & Commentary:

Introduced in 2024, the Rocha is a close cousin to the iconic Attache. The Attache is popular for use with belay devices as well as a general medium-capacity utlity carabiner for top-managed belays, personal anchoring etc. Introduced alongside the 4th generation Attache (now with a round stock basket), the Rocha is essentially a smaller, lighter Attache designed to take over the utility functions which have made the Attache so popular.

With increasingly smaller climbing ropes, the small size of the Rocha is not as much of a hassle as it would have been 10-20 years ago. It seems to nail the balance between useful gate opening & basket size, weight and handling. Additionally, the orange color will help distinguish it at a glance from all the yellow Attaches.

The Rocha includes a small attachment hole near the hinge, similar to the ones introduced on the Sm'D. This hole is for attaching a short tether to another device which must be removed from a carabiner to be attached to the rope, such as a Traxon, Tibloc or even modified Grigri. The upper holes on the nose are purely for weight reduction (and style) according to Petzl, and should not be used for tethers.
